Nikko Jenkins Biography

Nikko Jenkins is an American spree killer who was born and raised in Omaha, Nebraska, the United States. He was found culpable for committing four murders in Omaha, Nebraska, in August 2013. The murders took place within a month after he had been set free from prison after serving 10-and-a-half years in prison. He had been sentenced for a carjacking committed at the age of 15 and also for assaults committed in prison.

Nikko admitted the murder charges leveled against him. He was found fit to stand trial, found guilty of the four killings, and was sentenced to death in May 2017.

Nikko Murders

A patrol officer found two bodies in a white Ford pickup truck parked near a city swimming pool at 18th and F St, in Spring Lake Park on 11 August 2013. The two victims who had been shot in the head were identified as Jorge C. Cajiga-Ruiz and Juan Uribe-Pena. The two men were lured there hoping that they would be able to have sexual relations with two women.

The body of Curtis Bradford was discovered outside a detached garage at 18th and Clark St. on 19 August 2013 at around 7 a.m. The body was found by a man returning home from a night shift at a convenience store. Two bullet wounds were found in Bradford’s back.

Nikko’s fourth victim, Andrea Kruger, was found on 21 August 2013, at around 2:15 a.m. The body was discovered by a deputy sheriff responding to a shots-fired call. It was found lying in the road at 168th and Fort St., with several 12-gauge shotgun wounds to the face, neck, and shoulder.

Nikko Trial

After being found competent to stand trial,  the proceedings against him began. Upon his request, Nikko was allowed to represent himself at trial, under the guidance of advisory attorneys. Throughout his trial, Nikko maintained that he committed the killings at the command of  Apophis, a deity in the ancient Egyptian religion. He was found guilty of all four murders and was sentenced to death by a three-judge panel in May 2017.

He was also sentenced to 450 years in prison on weapons charges connected with the murders., the US Supreme Court refused to hear his appeal on 20 April 2020

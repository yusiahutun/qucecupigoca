Before joining the university, he went to Gardena High School of Harbor Gateway, Los Angeles, and graduated in 1972.

Later, he joined Pepperdine University in Malibu, California, for his master's degree in education. He also holds a J.D. degree from the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA).

Career

After graduating from California State University, the radio host became a high school teacher. He worked at Gage Middle School in Huntington Park, California, where he taught economics, history, and geography.

Legal career

The attorney's legal practise began when he was admitted to the California Bar on 4th December 1990. The same year, he became a member of the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP). He did pro-bono work for the organisation.

The attorney left the organisation after a dispute arose between him and NAACP for supporting Carol Kuhl. Kuhl was George W. Bush's nominee for the county judge of Los Angeles.

At some point, he chaired the Black-Korean alliance. He also served as an Advisory Board Member for the United States Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) and was a member of the Statewide Commission Against Hate Crimes.

Leo Terrell made significant contributions in his legal career, especially in civil rights. In 1995, he represented Kumasi Simmons, who was expelled from Centennial High School in Compton for physically assaulting the referee.

Simmons reportedly attacked the referee for using racial epithets directed at him. The attorney said the Beverly Hills Police Department intimidated witnesses who could have provided evidence for Simmons' claim.

In 1999, he advocated for the Los Angeles police commission to listen to witnesses who stated that Margaret Laverne Mitchell, a homeless lady, was shot by the police as she was running. She did not pose any threat to the police when she got shot.

In 2012, he called for the relevant authorities to investigate misconduct by trainees at the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department.

Besides advocating for civil rights, the attorney has offered legal commentary on various radio and television programmes. These include Good Morning America, Today, Larry King Live, Hannity & Colmes, Nightline, and The O'Reilly Factor.

Media career

The attorney began his radio career on 3rd June 1996, when he co-hosted the talk show called Terrell and Katz. Burton Katz, a former supreme court judge, was his co-host.

The weekday show aired on KMPC radio in Los Angeles, California. In the show, Terrell was the liberal voice, while Katz was the conservative one. On 5th October 1996, the show moved to KABC, where it aired on weekends.

He hosted the show on KABC until 15th August 2010. Since then, he has been a recurring guest host on The Peter Tilden Show on KABC. He also hosts a daily evening drive show called Leo 2.0 Live at Five on the same radio station.

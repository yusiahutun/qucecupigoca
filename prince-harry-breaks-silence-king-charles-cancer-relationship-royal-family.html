Prince Harry Breaks Silence on King's Cancer, Relationship With Family

March 31, 2024
blog

Prince Harry has broken his silence on his dad’s recent cancer diagnosis and his current relationship with the rest of the royal family.

Today, while preparing for the 2025 Invictus Games launch in Whistler, Canada, the Duke of Sussex told Good Morning America’s Will Reeve: “Umm, look, I love my family.” And of visiting father King Charles III: “The fact that I was able to get on a plane and go and see him and spend any time with him, I’m grateful for that.”

As Bazaar reported, Harry jumped on a plane from California to London immediately upon learning of father Charles’s cancer diagnosis last week. There, he met with the monarch for about an hour before returning home. Bazaar learned that Charles very much appreciated Harry’s gesture.

“I’ve also found in my own life that sort of an illness in the family can have a galvanizing or a sort of reunifying effect for a family,” Reeve said. “Is that possible in this case?'”

“Absolutely. Yeah, I’m sure,” Harry replied. “Throughout all these families I see it on a day-to-day basis, again, the strength of the family unit coming together. I think any illness, any sickness brings families together. I see it time and time again, and that makes me very happy.”

Harry’s answer comes as he and older brother Prince William remain estranged since Harry and wife Duchess Meghan stepped down as senior royals in 2020. Bazaar learned the two did not meet during Harry’s brief visit to London earlier this month.

Still, asked when he plans to go back to London to see his royal family, Harry said, “I have my own family, as we all do, right? My family and my life in California is as it is. I have got other trips planned that would take me through the U.K. or back to the U.K. I will stop in and see my family as much as I can.”

Elsewhere in his interview with GMA, Harry noted that he has thought about becoming a U.S. citizen now that he, Meghan, and their two kids—Prince Archie, four, and Princess Lilibet, two—are living full time in Montecito, California. Life in the U.S. with his little family is “amazing,” he said said. “I love every single day.”

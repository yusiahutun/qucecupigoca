Salmon is one of my favourite fish to cook; it's versatile, easily available and full of good fats. However, I often find myself falling into the same old routine with its preparation; my go-tos are salmon miso soup, poached in lemon juice, or just simply baked with some vegetables. 

This recipe is a decadent and easy way to shake things up. Just perfect for a cosy evening in.

Enjoy, Katie x

Seared Salmon with Caviar Sauce

Cooking time: 30 minutes

Serves: Two

Ingredients

2 fillets salmon

1 tbsp. butter

250g fresh greens

Caviar Sauce

1 cup and 2 tbsp. white wine/Prosecco

2 tbsp. cold water

2 shallots

1 lemon- juice

60g butter- cold

1 tbsp. cream

2 tbsp. caviar

Method

Get started by preparing your sauce; dice the shallots and pop them into a heavy saucepan with a cup of wine and lemon juice, and place over a medium/high heat.

Allow the cook until wine has almost completely reduced, and then add the extra wine and the water.

Slowly introduce the butter and whisk into the sauce.  Do this little by little to ensure the sauce doesn't separate.

Once all your butter has been added, add the cream little by little and whisk in.

Now stir in the caviar.

Next start on the salmon; heat a frying pan with the butter, and place over a medium heat on the hob.

Place the salmon skin side down and allow to cook.

When the skin has browned, and the fish has cooked on that side (about 5-6 minutes), turn over and cook the other side for about 3-4 minutes.

To make your greens, chop into even slices and then pop into a saucepan with a tbsp. or 2 of water. 

Cover and allow to cook for a couple of minutes, until wilted and tender (not soft!).

Serve the greens with the salmon and sauce on top.  

Enjoy!

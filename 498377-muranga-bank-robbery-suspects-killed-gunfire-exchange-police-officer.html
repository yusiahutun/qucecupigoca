One of the two suspected bank robbers who were killed after allegedly raiding Unaitas SACCO in Murang'a county has been identified.

Joseph Kamau, who was a former police officer, succumbed to gunshot injuries after a heavy fire exchange between the cops and his accomplices.

It was also alleged that the 30-year-old was killed by police officers, as initial photos show he and the other suspect, who is yet to be identified, cornered and their hands firmly tied.

Shot nine times

An autopsy conducted on Kamau's body established that he was shot at least nine times, three times in the head and six in the chest and abdomen, KBC reported.

Speaking to journalists, Kamau's father, Francis Kang’ethe, said he was shocked to learn of his son's killing.

Kang’ethe said he learnt of his death through social media. He implored investigation agencies to hasten the probe into the incident to unravel the mysterious circumstances under which he died.

The Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) detectives said they recovered firearms and KSh 1.13 million from Kamau and his dead accomplice.

Suspected robbers killed by their accomplices

Speaking on Tuesday, March 14, Central Regional Commissioner Fredrick Shishia defended the police against allegations that they killed the two suspects.

Shisia said the deceased were murdered by their fellow suspected robbers during a crossfire that ensured as the duo were taking the officers to their hideout in the expansive Kinyona Forest.

"After the arrest, the officers insisted that they show them where the firearms were and in the process, they had to go back to the forest to assist the officers to trace it. Unfortunately, as the officers were moving in the thicket looking for the possible location of the guns, they were met by gunfire from other robbers that had escaped. I think this is what prompted the officers to take cover and engage the other group in the exchange of fire. It is in the process that the two other suspects were fatally injured. The issue that the two suspects were killed by our officers is not true," said Shishia.

Their bodies were taken to Murang'a Level V Hospital mortuary awaiting identification.

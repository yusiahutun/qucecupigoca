Mysore Zoo is one of the oldest and most famous zoos in India.

Located in the city of Mysore in Karnataka, it was established in 1892 and is spread over an area of 157 acres. The zoo is home to a diverse range of animal species and is known for its significant contributions to wildlife conservation.

The zoo houses over 1500 animals belonging to more than 150 different species.

Visitors can encounter majestic elephants, fierce lions, playful orangutans, graceful giraffes, and many other fascinating creatures.

Mysore Zoo has successfully bred several endangered species.

The zoo actively participates in various breeding programs for endangered animals, including the Indian white-rumped vulture, the Sumatran orangutan, and the African cheetah.

The zoo is renowned for its breeding program of the rare and endangered Indian gharial.

These ancient reptiles can be found in the zoo’s dedicated gharial breeding center, which aims to increase their dwindling population in the wild.

Mysore Zoo offers a unique and educational experience with its walk-through aviary.

Visitors can marvel at various bird species living in a natural habitat, providing an up-close encounter with colorful parrots, exotic pheasants, and beautiful peacocks.

The zoo provides a natural breeding habitat for Asian elephants.

Mysore Zoo has implemented successful breeding programs to support the conservation of these magnificent creatures, which are considered an integral part of Indian culture.

Mysore Zoo is known for its beautiful and well-maintained gardens.

Visitors can enjoy the tranquil surroundings while exploring the zoo’s various enclosures and exhibits.

The zoo plays a crucial role in public education and awareness.

Through interactive exhibits, educational programs, and guided tours, Mysore Zoo aims to instill a sense of responsibility and promote conservation efforts among visitors.

Mysore Zoo has been actively involved in wildlife research and conservation.

The zoo collaborates with reputed scientific institutions to study animal behavior, habitat preservation, and captive breeding techniques.

Mysore Zoo is a popular tourist attraction, drawing thousands of visitors each year.

Its rich biodiversity and well-maintained facilities make it a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts and animal lovers.

The zoo is dedicated to providing excellent care and welfare for all its animals.

Special attention is given to the diet, medical care, and overall well-being of the animals to ensure their health and happiness.

Mysore Zoo actively participates in various international captive breeding programs.

By exchanging animals with other zoos around the world, it contributes to the genetic diversity and sustainability of endangered species.

The zoo offers a range of recreational activities for visitors.

In addition to exploring the animal exhibits, visitors can also enjoy boating, toy train rides, and picnicking in the picturesque Karanji Lake area within the zoo premises.

Mysore Zoo has a dedicated veterinary hospital.

The hospital ensures the timely treatment and care of sick or injured animals, further emphasizing the zoo’s commitment to animal welfare.

Mysore Zoo provides ample opportunities for wildlife photography.

The picturesque surroundings, coupled with a diverse range of animals, make it a paradise for shutterbugs.

FAQs

1. What are the operating hours of Mysore Zoo?

The zoo is open every day from 8:30 am to 5:30 pm.

2. How much is the entry fee for Mysore Zoo?

The entry fee for adults is Rs. 60, and for children (5-12 years), it is Rs. 30.

3. Is photography allowed inside the zoo?

Yes, photography is permitted inside the zoo, but using flash is not allowed.

4. Are there any guided tours available?

Yes, guided tours are available at an additional cost. These tours provide a more in-depth understanding of the various animal species and their habitats.

5. Is there a parking facility at Mysore Zoo?

Yes, there is a parking area near the zoo entrance where visitors can park their vehicles.

6. Are there any eating options available inside the zoo?

Yes, there are several food stalls and snack counters within the zoo premises that offer a variety of refreshments and snacks.

7. Can we bring our own food and water?

Outside food and water are not allowed inside the zoo for the safety and well-being of the animals. However, there are designated picnic areas outside the zoo where visitors can enjoy their own food.

8. Are there any wheelchair facilities for disabled visitors?

Yes, the zoo provides wheelchair facilities for disabled visitors. It is advisable to contact the zoo authorities in advance for any special requirements.

9. How long does it take to explore the entire zoo?

The time required to explore the zoo depends on individual preferences. On average, it takes around 3-4 hours to see all the exhibits and attractions.

10. Is Mysore Zoo suitable for young children?

Yes, Mysore Zoo is a family-friendly destination with special attractions and educational programs designed for young children.

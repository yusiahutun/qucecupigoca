One of two brothers who killed five members of his own family 'tried to attack prison staff'

April 08, 2024
blog

One of two brothers serving multiple life sentences for stabbing five members of their own family to death in 2015 is now accused of trying to attack prison staff with an eight inch long shank. 

Robert Bever approached two workers at the Joseph Harp Correctional Center in Lexington on Monday with the 'sharpened instrument', according to an Oklahoma Department of Corrections incident report.  

He came at them from behind with the weapon before one of the employees held him in a bear hug and told him to drop the weapon, The Tulsa World reports. 

The social services specialist then reportedly ordered Bever to the ground before he was restrained by security. 

Robert, then 18, and his brother Michael Bever, then 16, stabbed their parents and three siblings to death in July 2015 in a middle-of-the-night rampage in Tulsa.

Robert pleaded guilty in 2016 and was sentenced to life in prison without parole. His younger brother, Michael Bever was convicted in May of five counts of first-degree murder and sentenced to five life terms in prison to run consecutively.

The pair were arrested after their 13-year-old sister who survived the attack identified them as the attackers in the massacre in Broken Arrow. 

One of the victims, their brother Daniel, 12, had called 911 just before dying alongside his parents, David, 52, and April, 44, and siblings Christopher, seven, and five-year-old Victoria.

Their sister, Autumn Bever, then two, was discovered unharmed. 

Robert - who tried to hang himself in 2017 in the Tulsa County jail - will now face a record of misconduct coded as 'Inmate on Staff Assault without Serious Injury'.

He gave police a full confession saying he carried out the murders. He was apparently 'very forthcoming' during his confession but showed 'no remorse' for the bloodthirsty massacre. 

After the stabbings, the brothers reportedly had 3,000 rounds of ammunition delivered to their home which they planned to use to kill as many people as possible so that they could become as famous as Columbine shooters Dylan Klebold and Eric Harris.

Michael Bever, then 16, told Broken Arrow Police Detective Eric Bentz in a videotaped interrogation that his older brother Robert was motivated by a hatred of 'everyone' and a desire to become 'famous', while he only wanted to 'get a learner's permit'. 

Police called the slaughter the 'worst criminal event' in the community's history.

Neighbors said the mother stayed at home to home school the children, who were not allowed to play with other children in the neighborhood.  

During his trial, prosecutors heard how Michael tricked his siblings into opening locked doors as they hid from Robert by pretending that he too was under attack.

When police arrived at the family home, there was blood on the porch.

Michael and Robert had fled to the woods behind the house and were found by a police dog.

The brothers had planned to steal their parents' car and drive to Washington State after carrying out the killings.

They wanted to shoot five people at a time in random attacks along the way using guns and ammunition Robert had been able to buy online.

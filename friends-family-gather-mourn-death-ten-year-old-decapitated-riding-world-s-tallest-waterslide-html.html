Friends and family mourn the boy who was decapitated riding world's tallest waterslide

March 08, 2024
blog

The father of Caleb Schwab, who was killed while riding the world's tallest water slide, has paid an emotional tribute to his ten-year-old son. 

Scott Schwab and his wife Michele were seen leaving a reception for around 300 people on Friday following Caleb's funeral which was open to the public and live-streamed.

The father-of-four boys carried his youngest son to a limousine around 6.30pm and hugged fellow mourners before setting off. He was closely followed by his wife who shielded her eyes with sunglasses.

The parents of Caleb Schwab (left, Michelle and right Scott Schwab, with one of their other sons) were pictured together for the first time since losing their ten-year-old son.

Michele Scott was seen leaving the service wearing sunglasses, a black and white embroidered dress and monochrome shoes

Michele Schwab was accompanied by friends and family as she left the church where mourners were greeted by a montage of pictures and video of Caleb as they entered the auditorium

Around 300 mourners gathered at the LifeMission Church for the funeral service for 10-year-old Caleb Schwab

Caleb Schwab was killed on Sunday on the Verruckt waterslide at Schlitterbahn Waterpark in Kansas City

Friends and family had gathered on Friday to mourn the death of Caleb at a public service at Life Mission Church in Olathe, Kansas, where his parents and three brothers are active members.

Mourners at the service were greeted by a montage of pictures and video of Caleb as they entered the auditorium, including cellphone video clips of the ten-year-old, laughing and chatting to the camera. 

Photos showed the boy playing his favorite sports of baseball and soccer, cuddling with his three brothers on the couch and pulling funny faces. There were also touching shots of the family together on vacations and carving pumpkins.

During the ceremony, Caleb's father Scott Schwab, a Kansas State Representative, gave a moving eulogy to his son who he described as his 'joy.'

'When my kids were born, Nathan was my pleasure, Caleb was my joy, Alex was my delight and Isaac was my treasure. We've lost some joy. We've lost Caleb, my joy. But we'll get it back,' he said.  

Caleb Schwab's father, Kansas state Rep Scott Schwab, spoke at his ten-year-old son's funeral on Sunday

Caleb was killed on Sunday on the Verruckt waterslide at Schlitterbahn Waterpark in Kansas City

Caleb's father and older brother, Nathan, stood at the front of Life Mission Church in Olathe, Kansas, during the boy's funeral

Nearly a week after Caleb (pictured right with his parents and three brothers) was thrown from the waterslide and decapitated, friends and family are still coming to terms with the boy's death

Caleb's father Scott Schwab's brother, Rick Schwab, who is a pastor in Michigan, gave a moving tribute to his nephew saying he wished he had been able to spend more time with him and said that Jesus had caught Caleb in his arms at the bottom of the waterslide.

Caleb's mother, Michele, tightly held her eldest son, Nathan, as the family stood on the front row of a packed auditorium at Life Mission.

The stage was adorned with large floral tributes and a portrait of the smiling ten-year-old.  

The youngster was on the Kansas City Mudcats baseball team and his fellow players sat on the front row in their uniforms to say goodbye to their friend.

Caleb's father, Scott Schwab, paid tribute to them for helping shape his son's character and being there for him. He asked the congregation to rise to applaud them.

'You guys are awesome,' he told them.

'Caleb's friends, thank you for helping shape his character and building memories that we will cherish forever. You guys are awesome,' Schwab said. 'If it wasn't for a lot of you guys, you Mudcats and Orange Crush and Thunder, we'd have less trophies in the basement, but you guys were a part of his life and you guys were great and a lot of the heartache that we burden is knowing that you're hurting too.

'And again, like I said last night to most of you, our goal is to get to a place where we think of Caleb and we have joy, we don't have sorrow for what we lost. And we're committed to getting there together,' Scott Schwab said. 

He also explained the devastating impact that the loss of his son Caleb had on his family.

Caleb was an avid athlete, having played baseball for the Mudcats in Lenexa. His friends and teammates sat at in the front row of his funeral

He said his younger boys, Isaac and Alex, had been left without an older brother, while he addressed his eldest, saying: 'Nathan, it's not the way I wanted you to enter your manhood but you've done very well.

'I am humbled to call you my son. 

He also shared a touching story about the time he had lost his job and Caleb showed understanding beyond his years.

Schwab was breaking the news to his sons and said his eldest, Nathan, was concerned asking if they would lose their car, or if he would have to sell his son.

But Caleb, who was just five at the time, simply turned to his father and said: 'Dad, I'm so sorry that happened to you.

'I was the kid who scraped the knee at that moment and he held me. That was Caleb.' 

The grieving father placed a kiss on this son's portrait before making his way to the stage.

He also thanked the Kansas governor who was in attendance along with those in the local legislature, the close-knit community, his family and the moms of Caleb's sports teams for their great show of support.

As he left the stage he asked those who had ever been on a field with Caleb join him at the front for a huddle, which ended with a chant of Caleb's name. 

Nearly a week after he was thrown from the raft on the world's tallest waterslide into the safety netting and decapitated, friends and family members are still coming to terms with the boy's death.

Caleb was supposed to start fifth grade this upcoming week at Heritage Christian Academy, where administrators are focusing on helping his friends and classmates cope.

Caleb's baseball teammates wore their uniforms to the funeral to honor his love of the sport

Caleb's parents (his father pictured above) and brothers are active members of Mission Life Church, where his funeral was held

'We're going to treasure him in our hearts and figure out ways to make our time we had with him more meaningful,' Heritage Christian school administrator Rick Lukianuk told KCTV. 'Caleb's never going to be forgotten.' 

After the ceremony, Scott Schwab asked the funeral congregation to join his family at the Ball Conference Center in Olathe, Kansas, a short distance from their Life Mission Church.

He said the family were hoping to meet with many more people who had supported them after a visitation for Caleb was overwhelmed on Thursday evening, with hundreds of people unable to share their condolences directly with the grief-stricken couple. 

Caleb was the second oldest of his siblings. His older brother Nathan was planning on riding the waterslide with Caleb, but they were told they didn't weigh enough to go together.

According to Schlitterbahn Kansas City, rafts sliding down the 17-story waterslide must have two to three riders that together weigh between 400 and 550 pounds to prevent the raft from going airborne before it makes a 168-foot vertical drop.

The were weighed on a scale at the bottom of the waterslide and again at the top and were told that they couldn't ride the raft together because even with a third person, the combined weight of all three riders wouldn't hid 400 pounds.

So Nathan went first - along with two adult strangers - and waited for his brother to come down the slide behind him. 

The church streamed a photo collage of photos of Caleb as part of the service and showed the funeral live online

Caleb was supposed to start fifth grade at Heritage Christian Academy this upcoming week 

The ten-year-old has three brothers (all four brothers pictured with their mother, Michele, above). Caleb was the second oldest of his siblings

But instead, he watched from below as a horrific scene unfolded before his eyes.

Witnesses described gruesome scenes at the theme park, saying they saw the boy fly out of the raft, through the harness, and hit the safety netting above where he was beheaded.

Caleb was found decapitated in the pool at the end of the ride, and an investigation is currently underway into what happened. 

The boys' father is Kansas state Rep Scott Schwab. Kansas House Speaker Ray Merrick sent an email to colleagues on Sunday asking for prayers for the Schwab family, according to ABC.

'It is with an absolutely broken heart we have to ask for prayers for the Schwab family, who suddenly and tragically lost their sweet and energetic son in an accident today,' he wrote. 'No words can express the pain we all feel for this terrible tragedy.'

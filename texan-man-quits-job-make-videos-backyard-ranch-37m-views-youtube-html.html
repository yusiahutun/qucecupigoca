Texan man quits job to make videos of his backyard ranch and now has over 37m views on YouTube

September 05, 2024
blog

What started as 'buying some chickens on impulse' as a college student, for Ben Christie, 24, from Austin, Texas, has now become a thriving non-profit venture with millions of fans.

The social media star, who goes by Uncle Ben, creates 20 to 30-minute videos about an average day on his backyard ranch. 

He feeds his wild Rheas Kevin and Karen (who have just welcomed their first son), plays with a kangaroo called Da Baby (named after the hip-hop artist) and goes about cleaning chores with his dog Poggers.

He also eats raw ostrich eggs, sticks his hand in an ant hill and goes to public parks at night to rescue ducks from ponds.

Ben admits that he doesn't come from a farming or animal rehabilitation background and that the idea to start up The Urban Rescue Ranch came from a joke with his college roommate at Baylor University who had a puppy which 'would just poop in his closet everyday'.

Ben said: 'I told him 'Dude, 'I could get a whole farm of animals and they would poop less than this puppy.'

He 'didn't know anything about farming animals' but naively thought 'how hard could this be?'. Ben went on to buy six chicks and two ducklings, which is where his love for rescue truly began.

The chicks grew up and went on to lay eggs which Ben sold at his college, walking around the campus library with a little duck.

He said: 'People knew me as 'the chicken guy' for a bit.'

That's when people started coming to him with animals that needed help.

While Ben was passionate about animal rehabilitation and farming, it wasn't until the social media success the ranch gained at the start of the pandemic that he took the 'leap of faith' to make it a full-time job.

After graduating from college he began working in sales at software company Oracle. After Covid, Ben was working from home while producing videos and TikToks about the ranch.

He said: 'I was doing my calls and meetings while bottle-feeding little deer. After a while I realised that what I'm really passionate about is the animal stuff. Even my boss at Oracle would be saying things like 'when are you going to quit and just do the ranch full-time'.'

He said: 'When Covid started TikTok was really popping off and we started to get a lot of viewers. Then we started making videos on YouTube and then we started doing things like Cameo.'

To raise money, Ben was also selling Ayam Cemani chicken eggs. These chickens, fully black in colour including their bones and internal organs, go for $100 apiece.

Ben said: 'When you're getting 180 eggs half of those are hatching, you're making decent money.

'I realised I was essentially stealing from my company and wasn't working as hard as I should be. I was looking at the animal stuff and thinking 'what would happen if I put my life into that'.' The day after Ben quit his job, a video of his called 'How To Survive A Rhea Attack' 'blew up'. It now has 5.6 million views.

Ben, who admits he does not come from a farming background, has more than 2.9 million followers and 78.7 million likes on TikTok

'Every other video then started doing well,' he said. 'And that's why these videos have been one of the biggest blessings to us because on TikTok you can get 14 or 15 million views on something but you don't make money from that. With YouTube we could show people more.'

Apart from YouTube ad revenue, the ranch also makes money from Cameo, Patreon, merch sales and donations.

Now, Ben is 'working harder than he ever was' and The Urban Rescue Ranch has plans to move to a three-acre property in Waco, Texas.

All of the renovations, totalling at some $30,000, have cost the ranch. This means that even though it's made money, most of it is going into expanding.

Ben said: 'Considering the debt for the new land in Waco and the renovations and fencing and waterlines, we need to fill a $150,000 gap to be completely debt-free and 100 per cent.

'That's why we're selling merch and regularly uploading on YouTube. We're pending for non-profit status at the moment and fixing up the new ranch. It's been a crazy ride and I'm extremely thankful for all of it.'

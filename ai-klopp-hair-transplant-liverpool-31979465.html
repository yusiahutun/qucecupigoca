Jurgen Klopp is one of many men in football that have gone under the knife to save their much-loved hairlines.

The Liverpool boss - who stunningly announced he'll be leaving the Premier League giants at the end of the season - had a hair transplant in 2012, three years before arriving at Anfield.

While the German, 56, possessed long locks for much of his reign at Bundesliga side Borussia Dortmund, it was merely a strategy to hide his receding hairline.

He began sporting a noticeably thicker head of hair during his last years in Germany as he admitted to having a hair transplant during an interview with German publication Bild. The enigmatic football manager even asked the opinions of reporters on how it looked.

"Yes, it’s true. I underwent a hair transplant,” Klopp told journalists. "And I think the results are really cool, don’t you?" According to experts, the 56-year-old is believed to have undergone a Follicular Unit Excision (FUE) hair transplant procedure.

The method involves removing individual clusters of hair follicles before implanting them in thinning or balding areas of the scalp. However, thanks to the wonders of AI, the public are able to witness what Klopp would look like if he never underwent the procedure.

The startling snap shows the German sporting a barely-there quiff at the top of his forehead with a freshly shaven look elsewhere on his head.

Griffin Alexander Gluck, commonly known as Griffin Gluck, is a vicenarian American actor. He is best known for his various roles such as Charlie in the “Red Band Society” series, Danny Gannon in “Back in the Game” and many more.

Early Life

Griffin Gluck was born on the 24th of August, 2000 in Los Angeles, California, United States to film director and producer Cellin Gluck and a production assistant and line producer Karin Beck. He has one elder sister named Caroline Paris Gluck who is also an actress. Reportedly, he attended Oak Park High School located in Oak Park, California. He is of Japanese and Austrian Jewish descent from his paternal side and of English, German, Scottish, Polish, and Irish ancestry from his maternal side.

Griffin Gluck Career

It was the year 2009 when Gluck made his debut in the television industry by working in the film “Sideways aka Saidoweizu” as a Pharmacy boy. In 2010, he appeared as Half Bred in one episode: “WUPHF.com” of a series “The Office”. In 2011, he did a starring role of Michael Murphy in a romantic comedy film “Just Go For It”. He also did two television series namely, “The United States of Tara”, where he was cast in 3 episodes as Monty and “Private Practice”, where he did the main role of Mason Warner from seasons 5 to 6. In 2013, he did one film named “Trust Me” as Philip and one series named “Back in the Game”, where he did a starring role of Danny Gannon. In 2014, he appeared as Randy Morgan in the film “Just Before I Go”.

He also did the role of Adderall boy in 1 episode of the series “Silicon Valley”. In the same year, he played the lead role of Charlie in a teen medical comedy-drama series “Red Band Society”. In 2015, he did a voice-over of Young Bruce Wayne in the film “Batman vs. Robin”. He also did 3 series namely, “About a Boy” in 3 episodes as Clay; “Cuckoo” in a TV pilot as Dylan; “Life in Pieces” as Aiden in one episode.

In 2017, he played the main role of Sam Ecklund in a mockumentary series “American Vandal”. In 2019, he appeared as Monroe “Mo” Harris in a coming-of-age comedy film “Big Time Adolescence”. He also did the role of Jack Dunkleman in the film “Tall Girl”. In 2020, he appeared in the main role of Gabe in the series “Locke & Key”. He also did a recurring role of Aaron in the series “The Real Bros of Simi Valley”.

Awards and nominations

Griffin was nominated at the 33rd Young Artist Awards in the “Best Performance in a Feature Film- Supporting Young Actor” category for his work in the film named “Just Go With It”.

Maina Njenga Surrenders to Court Days after Declared Him Wanted for Possessing Bhang

May 04, 2024
blog
  • Former Mungiki leader Maina Njenga went missing after police accused him of possessing 90 rolls of bhang and two firearms
  • Njenga presented himself to Nakuru Law Courts, where he will be charged with several offences
  • On Tuesday, May 23, the former Mungiki leader secured anticipatory bail, restraining the police from arresting and detaining him

Nakuru - Former Mungiki leader Maina Njenga has presented himself at the Nakuru Law Courts days after police launched a manhunt.

On Wednesday, May 24, the former Mungiki leader surrendered himself to the court accompanied by his lawyer Ndegwa Njiru.

Police attempts to arrest Njenga in court

Njenga is expected to be charged with various offences relating to being a member of an organised criminal group and engaging in organised criminal activities.

There was some drama after detectives attempted to arrest Njenga inside the courtroom.

It took the intervention of his lawyer, Njiru, who protested against the move, saying he will not allow his client to be arrested.

The development comes a day after the Laikipia politician got an anticipatory bail stopping police from arresting or detaining him for allegedly possessing two guns and 90 rolls of bhang.

Maina Njenga goes into hiding

Detectives from the Directorate of Criminal Investigations had launched a manhunt to Njenga over items allegedly found in a home linked to him.

"Detectives based in Nakuru County are looking for ex-Mungiki leader Maina Njenga, in relation to the recovery of two firearms and over 90 rolls of bhang, found at a home linked to him," read a statement by the DCI.

DCI had explained that during a raid conducted at home in Ngomongo village, they arrested two who were in possession of two guns.

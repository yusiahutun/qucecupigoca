The 20 Best Red Lipsticks of 2024, Tested and Reviewed

July 16, 2024
blog

Daring, bold, and a frequent conversation topic, red lipstick and Taylor Swift are eternal twin flames. And, though the shade has become one of her calling cards since she first swatched it on for an Allure photoshoot in 2009, it’s been essential in our makeup collection long before the once curly-haired pop star stepped on stage. We’re beholden to its timeless appeal (dare we say it never goes out of style?), and how a red lip can re-energize lackluster looks and inspire confidence in us the way a soft pink or brown shade simply can’t. 

Taylor Swift’s signature red lip has sparked an intense office-wide interest in the sport known as football — more specifically, the fun guessing game that ensues every Monday trying to guess what she wore. As imitation is the sincerest form of flattery, we tested some of the world’s most popular and iconic scarlet-hued lippies across a range of undertones, brightness and color blends, and finishes. We’ve completed our rigorous review, and we’ve curated our final list of winners. Our all-time favorite red lipsticks (and probably Taylor’s too) are featured in this list, providing you with the crucial red lipsticks you need in your repertoire. Let the games begin.

What To Keep in Mind 

  • Finish: When it comes to lipstick, professional makeup artist, Monica R, emphasizes that finding the right finish is vital to get the most out of your lipstick. Most lipsticks come in a matte, satin, or shine finish. If you’re looking for something long lasting, she recommends picking one with a matte finish (like the Sunnies Face Fluffmate) as they contain less moisture which makes them less transferable. If you have naturally drier lips, we recommend picking a hydrating lipstick with a shinier finish — one of our favorites is Tarte Maracuja Juicy Lipstick. Most lip products that have a shiny finish are naturally more moisturizing and longer lasting, as they are formulated with oils and vitamins. Another option (if you want the best of both worlds) is opting for a satin finish. This finish is great for those who want more of a natural look on their lips. ILIA Color Block High Impact Lipstick is one of the most comfortable satin finish lipstick around.
  • Shade: When choosing which shade of red to wear, R notes that it’s necessary to understand your skin’s undertones to find what reds look best. Naturally, cool-toned reds compliment fairer skin. On the other hand, if you have more of an olive skin tone (like R), orange and warm-toned reds complement it and will give you a healthy glow. Darker skin tones can handle brighter, more intense colors. If you have darker skin, opt for deeper burgundy hues. At the end of the day, you should wear what makes you happy and what you feel confident in. (And if you’re unsure of your undertone, opt for a universal red, like MAC Retro Matte Lipstick.)

Your Questions, Answered

How do you wear red lipstick?

“I think red lipstick is the perfect statement and can uplift our mood or confidence when we use it, so on those days that I feel a little down or I need a boost, it's a great choice!” says Mariela Bagnato, professional makeup artist and founder of MBeauty. One of her favorite ways to wear a matte red lip is to pair it with a striking cat eye. She also loves wearing “minimal makeup with dewy skin, a ponytail, and a pop of color just on the lips,” for an easy everyday look.

What's the best way to apply red lipstick?

When it comes to applying red lipstick, there’s quite a bit of prep work involved to ensure it goes on flawlessly. “I love to exfoliate my lips prior to applying any lip to remove dry skin,” Bagnato says. After exfoliation, apply a hydrating balm to lock in moisture. 

If you’re worried about the lipstick feathering or bleeding, R recommends using a budge-proof lip liner, to ensure the lipstick doesn’t move. Bagnato likes to dab creamier formulas with a tissue to remove any excess product that can transfer. Once applied and dried, R says to clean the edges with a concealer so the end result is clean and crisp.

How can you get red lipstick out of clothes?

Nothing is worse than getting red lipstick on your favorite white button down. Fortunately, there are ways to remedy any makeup mishaps. First and foremost, don’t ever rub, as you run the chance of spreading the product and having it bleed. Instead, use alcohol or a stain remover to blot the stain with a damp cloth. After dabbing for a few minutes, run your garment under cold water to rinse off any remnants.

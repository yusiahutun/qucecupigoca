Educational background

The best-selling author went to a local elementary school before proceeding to Middletown High School, a public high school near his home. He joined the U.S. Marine Corps after high school and served as a combat correspondent with the 2nd Marine Aircraft Wing in the Iraq War.

He went back to his home country and enrolled for higher studies at the Ohio State University. He graduated with a Bachelor of Arts degree in Political Science and Philosophy. He then proceeded to Yale Law School, where he earned a Juris Doctor degree.

Career

The politician started his career after high school in the U.S. Marine Corps. He worked for Republican Ohio State Senator Bob Schuler during his time at the Ohio State University.

After completing his education, the venture capitalist worked at a corporate law firm in his home area. He then relocated to San Francisco to work in tech. He was a principal at Mithril Capital, a renowned venture capital firm owned by Peter Thiel.

Towards the end of 2016, the venture capitalist announced his decision to relocate to Ohio. He hinted at the desire to run for office, run a non-profit organisation, and combat drug addiction in his home county.

After relocating, he joined Steve Case's investment firm, Revolution LLC. His primary duty was to expand the Rise of the Rest project.

The goal of the project was to grow investments in under-served regions outside the Silicon Valley and New York City tech zones. In January 2017, J.D. became a CNN contributor.

In 2019, he co-founded Narya Capital in Cincinnati. He has also invested in Rumble, a Canadian online video platform.

Authorship

During his first year at Yale, professor Amy Chua encouraged J.D. to write his memoir. In 2016, he published Hillbilly Elegy: A Memoir of a Family and Culture in Crisis.

The book became a best-seller in New York in 2016 and 2017 and was a finalist for the 2017 Dayton Literary Peace Prize. Hillbilly Elegy also won the 2017 Audie Award for Nonfiction.

The book gives his personal account of being raised in an impoverished white working-class 'hillbilly' community. The community experienced multiple social and economic crises. He also tells how he left the community and became the man he is today.

In April 2017, he and Ron Howard signed a deal to have a film based on Hillbilly Elegy: A Memoir of a Family and Culture in Crisis. Netflix released the film in 2020. It starred Owen Asztalos and Gabriel Basso.

Politics

J.D. started his political journey in early 2018. He allegedly considered a bid for U.S. Senate as a Republican but declined to run for the position. He expressed interest in the senatorial seat in April 2021.

His bid was financially supported by Peter Thiel and Robert Mercer. He joined the race in July 2021, making it his first time campaigning for a public office.

He won the Republican primary on 3rd May 2022. In the general election held on 8th November 2022, he beat Democratic nominee Tim Ryan by 6.1%. He is now serving as the junior United States senator from Ohio.

The politician is known as a populist conservative. He strongly opposes the termination of pregnancies, especially after 15 weeks and is against the Respect for Marriage Act.

While he feels that illegal immigration is dirty, he does not support demonising illegal immigrants.

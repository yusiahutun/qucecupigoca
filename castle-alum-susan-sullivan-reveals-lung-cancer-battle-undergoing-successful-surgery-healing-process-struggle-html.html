Susan Sullivan — known for her role on the ABC drama series Castle — has been battling lung cancer.

The actress, 80, informed fans of her diagnosis Tuesday and revealed that she recently underwent a 'successful' surgery.

'Thanks for all the lovely comments and concerns. I had lung cancer,' penned Sullivan on X, days after sharing a photo of herself in hospital.

She continued: 'The surgery was successful. The healing process is a struggle. Thanks glad you're out there!'

Sullivan did not share any further details about her cancer diagnosis or what her recovery will entail.

In a photo shared to X on Monday, Sullivan is seen standing beside her hospital bed in a dressing gown while attached to an IV drip and a heart rate monitor.

'Life's surprising little turns try to be ready for them with humor and hope. On we go,' she captioned the post.

Since sharing her diagnosis, Sullivan has received an outpouring of love and support from fans and friends. 

Sullivan has an acting career that spans over 60 years and includes roles on Broadway and television.

Her leading role in the medical drama Julie Farr, M.D. — which aired on ABC from 1978 to 1979 — earned her an Emmy nomination.

She became a Golden Globe nominee in 1999 for her supporting role in the hit ABC sitcom Dharma & Greg.

She played Kitty Montgomery, the aristocratic mother of Greg Montgomery (played by Thomas Gibson).

Sullivan is most recognized for her role as Maggie Gioberti in the primetime drama Falcon Crest that aired on CBS for a total of nine seasons from 1981 to 1990.

She also played a 'Maggie' while starring as Maggie Porter in the TV miniseries Rich Man, Poor Man Book II.

It ran from September 1976 to March 1977 with each one-hour episode being released weekly Tuesday nights on ABC.

It was the sequel to the miniseries Rich Man, Poor Man Book (1976) that also starred leading man Peter Strauss.

She's also well known for her appearances as Lenore Curtin Delaney on the soap opera Another World which aired on NBC from 1964 to 1999.

Early life

Naomi Campbell was born Naomi Elaine Campbell on 22 May 1970 in Lambeth, South London, England. She is the daughter of a Jamaican-born dancer, Valerie Morris. Unfortunately, she has never met her father, in accordance with her mother's wishes.

Campbell’s father abandoned her mother when she was four months pregnant and went unnamed on her birth certificate. Her mother remarried, forcing Naomi to take the surname Campbell from her stepfather.

The model has two half-brothers named Richard Blackwood and Pierre Blackwood. She grew up in Rome, Italy, where her mother worked as a modern dancer. She returned to London and lived with relatives while her mother travelled across Europe with the dance troupe Fantastica.

Naomi Campbell attended the Barbara Speake Stage School and later enrolled at the Italia Conti Academy of Theatre Arts, where she studied ballet. The former supermodel also studied at Dunraven School, a mixed, all-through school in Streatham, Greater London, England.

Farm tools and equipment: names, pictures and uses

February 01, 2024
blog

Farm machinery and tools help reduce the amount of effort you put on your farm. Furthermore, these essential items help improve production. An understanding of the different farm tools and equipment and their uses could increase your chances of success. As a witty farmer, you need to know all the essential farm implements that you should own.

Farm equipment aid different farming activities such as general tilling, getting rid of weeds, fumigation, and fertilizer application. But you first need to know the essential farm tools and equipment and their functions to know which ones you need most.

Crucial farm tools and equipment

Here are the essential farm tools and equipment and their uses with pictures:

Tape measure

If you wish to create long-lasting and stable farm structures, you need to be accurate. This important farm tool helps in taking measurements and ensuring precision when creating structures.

Sickle

This handheld farm tool with a curved blade comes in handy when you want to cut succulent forage for your livestock or harvest grain crops. Since the beginning of the Iron Age to recent times, there have been hundreds of variants of the simple tool on different locations across the globe. These, however, maintain two distinct patterns; either serrated or smooth.

You can use either to cut mature cereals or green grass. But the serrated variety is still the most ideal for grain harvesting.

Spring balance

The equipment comes in handy whenever you want to measure loads within the farm environment. This may include the weight of a bag of maize, animal hides, or even meat. Not only is the item easy to use, but it is also pocket-friendly and robust.

The pickaxe

The hand tool has a hard head that is also attached to a handle perpendicularly. A pickaxe is basically used to break up a hard surface, as a gardening implement, or for general landscaping work. The metal head has two ends; one is blunt while the other is pointed. These two sides are used for breaking up hard surfaces and prying things up, respectively.

One major advantage that a pickaxe has over a shovel is the fact that it can tear up more compact soils. With the sharp end, you can easily break up rocky soils or hardened clay.

Hoe/jembe

This farm handtool is among the most ancient and is also highly versatile. It can be used for shaping the soil, controlling weeds, clearing the soil, as well as for harvesting root crops like potatoes. Shaping the soil may involve gathering piles of soil below plants (also called hilling), creating drills, controlling weeds, and digging shallow trenches to plant bulbs or seeds.

The fork jembe, particularly, has sturdy tines that can help loosen, lift, and turn over compact soil.

Sprayer

The sprayer comes in handy when you want to apply fertilizers, herbicides, or pesticides. The indispensable farm tool comes in different sizes, ranging from man-portable units to the larger trailed sprayers attached to tractors to self-propelled equipment. Hand-operated sprayers come in different varieties, with most models essentially used for controlling agricultural pests.

This equipment comes with components such as calibration, pressure regulation, compound nozzles, and spray tips to maintain accuracy and consistency when spraying herbicides.

Secateurs

This is a pair of sharp, rigid scissors or snips able to cut through little branches of young trees or shrubs. Occasionally, they may be used for harvesting farm produce like large vegetables and fruits like grapes or peaches.

The rake

This is among the most popular farm tools equipment and implements and is available in almost every home. Just like many other hand devices, you can choose from a wide range of rakes, depending on your specific use. On the farm, this toothed bar serves the main purpose of loosening and leveling the ground. You can also use it to gather hay, fallen leaves, and dead grass, as well as for any work that may require a harrow.

Crowbar

Also, among the essential farming tools is the crowbar. This incredible tool has a variety of uses. For instance, you can use it to dig holes and to dig out rocks, stumps, and other objects stuck in the soil. Furthermore, it functions perfectly as a lever arm.

The wheelbarrow

This compact transporter can be used by a single person conveniently to transport different items across the farm. Wheelbarrow in Kiswahili translates to “ngolofimo,” “likwama,” or “toroli.” The close-parked truck is usually assembled with a single wheel and is the most convenient carrying tool for any lightweight and a few heavyweights on the farm.

You can use it moving animal waste, transporting fertilizers from storage to the fields, for carrying seeds. or as a toolbox when moving for a distance with several tools.

Hosepipe

When it comes to the transfer of water throughout the garden, then the hose pipe stands out. This important farm item aids the watering of plants in the field, lawn, or garden. Usually, it conveys water to a sprinkler for irrigation purposes

The axe

Also known as ax in American English, you can toss this all-purpose farm equipment in your backpack, car trunk, or on your wheelbarrow. The tool is mainly used to shape, split, and cut wood, and it comes in a variety of forms alongside different specialized uses. It generally comprises a handle and axe head that can be made of materials such as copper, steel, iron, and bronze.

When chopping wood, these are among the simplest garden tools that you can use, and they are classified either as splitting or cutting axes.

Watering can

This container is typically made of metal or plastic and has a spout on one end that has a bulged nozzle punctured with lots of holes. This shape enables it to adequately distribute water over plants and is the most ideal for small scale gardening.

Shovel

It would help if you had this crucial farm tool as it helps dig and move material from one point to the other, such as soil, grains, sand, or gravel. They are used more extensively in the farms, gardens, and on construction sites. Mostly, the hand tool consists of a wide blade attached to a handle of medium length.

Panga

A panga is among the key tools and equipment that you must come across in a shamba. It helps facilitate any form of cutting. This includes smaller trees, branches, dried up crops, and fodder. Furthermore, it can be used for pruning and trimming hedges.

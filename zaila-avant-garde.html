Basketball

Zaila Avant-garde began playing basketball at the age of five and quickly developed a passion for the sport. She has since won multiple titles and awards, including the 2018 USA Basketball 3-on-3 national championship and the 2019 National AAU title.

Spelling bee Champion

Meanwhile, she gained much fame as a spelling bee champion. She made history in July 2021 when she won the 93rd Scripps National Spelling Bee, becoming the first African American to win the competition in its 96-year history.

The spelling bee, which was held virtually due to the COVID-19 pandemic, featured 209 spellers ranging in age from 9 to 15 years old.

The Avant-garde, who had competed in the Scripps National Spelling Bee twice before, was considered a favorite to win after she made it to the finals in 2019.

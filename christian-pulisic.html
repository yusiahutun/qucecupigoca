Christian Pulisic | Early Life, Family & Education

Chelsea’s famous winger and attacking midfielder, Christian Pulisic, was born on September 18, 1998, in Hershey, Pennslyvania, to his proud parents, Mark Pulisic and Kelley Pulisic. He has a sister, Devyn Pulisic, and a stepbrother, Chase, who has autism. 

Pulisic grew up with parents who loved soccer and played soccer at George Mason University.

His father, Mark, played professionally for an indoor soccer team called the Harrisburg Heat during the 1990s.

After his retirement as an athlete, Christian’s father started working as a coach at the youth and professional levels.

Christian started playing soccer at the tender age of 7 with the youth soccer club of Brackley Town.

Later, he played for the Michigan Rush football club and attended Workman Elementary School after his father became the general manager of the indoor soccer club called the Detroit Ignition.

His family moved again and resided in the Hershey area, where he was born. He represented PA Classics, a local U.S. Soccer Development Academy.

Besides, Christian occasionally trained with a professional club called the Harrisburg City Islander, which is presently called Penn FC.

Christian Pulisic | Career & Profession

Borussia Dortmund 

Christian started his professional club career with Dortmund at 16 in February 2015. Initially, he was part of the U17 team, and later Dortmund promoted him to the U19 team.

Christian made his debut on January 24, 2016, in a friendly match against Union Berlin, where he scored a goal and made an assist while playing for 90 minutes.

Similarly, Pulisic made his debut in the UEFA Europa League while playing against FC Porto on February 18, 2016.

His first Bundesliga game was on February 21, 2016, when the club competed against Bayer Leverkusen.

After his second appearance in the Bundesliga, the team manager stated-

“Christian is just a teenager in his first year of professional football. His first two games as the starting eleven were with Leverkusen and here today in Schalke – which is not the easiest of tasks.

It presents our huge appreciation…..see him as a full-time player on our team… He looked outstanding lately, which was proven today. It is completely normal not to have played with such pace and intensity for over 90 minutes.”

Christian scored his career-first Bundesliga goal on April 17, making him the fourth-youngest and youngest non-German player to score a goal at just 17.

He also became Bundesliga’s youngest player to score two top-flight league goals on April 23, 2016.

Further

Christian became Dortmund’s youngest (17 Years 11 Months) player to compete in the UEFA Champions League on September 14, 2016.

He signed a contract extension with Dortmund on January 23, 2017. According to the contract, Christian had to play for the club until 2020.

Pulisic’s first goal in the UEFA Champions League made him the youngest scorer of Dortmund in the league. Additionally, he recorded his first DFL- Supercup goal against Bayern Munich on August 5, 2017.

Chelsea

On January 2, 2019, Christian was signed by Chelsea FC on a deal worth $73 million, which made him the most expensive American player. He was also the second most expensive player sold by Dortmund.

However, he was again loaned to his previous club Borussia Dortmund. On June 30, 2019, after the end of his loan period, he returned to Chelsea.

Debut Season

Christian made his Premier League debut against Manchester United on August 11, 2019.

He scored his first goal as a Chelsea player on October 26, 2019, finishing with a ‘perfect’ hat-trick in a win against Burnley.

His perfect hat-trick record was the first of his career, and Christian became the second American to accomplish this in the Premier League.

Christian was also the youngest hat-trick scorer in entire Chelsea history. He became the first Chelsea member to score a perfect hat trick since 2010.

Christian Pulisic | Injury

On August 1, 2010, Christian scored the opening goal during the 2020 FA Cup Final while playing against Arsenal and became the first American player to score in the competition finals.

However, Pulisic was substituted early during the second half due to a hamstring injury.

In August 2020, Christian became part of the eight-player shortlists for the Premier League Young Player of the Season honors, which was later awarded to Trent Alexander-Arnold of Liverpool.

After recovering from injury, Christian made his season debut as a substitute on October 3, 2020 and played his first Premier League match of the season as a starting 11 against Southampton on October 17.

During the game against Leeds United on December 5, 2020, Christian’s 93rd-minute goal for Chelsea made him the fastest American to score ten Premier League goals.

He tested positive for Covid-19 in 2021 and did not play until November, as he suffered from an ankle injury.

On January 2, 2022, he scored an equalizer in a 2-2 draw against Liverpool. They won under the supervision of Graham Potter, Chelsea’s new manager, against Wolverhampton. 

Christian Pulisic | International Career

Besides playing professional soccer for different local and international clubs, Christian has also represented the United States senior, U-15, and U-17 teams. He was eligible to play internationally, representing Croatia, but he declined the opportunity.

He was the captain of the U-17 team during the 2015 FIFA U-17 World Cup. 

On March 27, 2016, the national senior team’s head coach, Jürgen Klinsmann, called Pulisic to represent the national team during World Cup qualifiers.

Thus, making Christian the youngest American player to play in the World Cup qualifier. He also became part of the senior team for the Copa América Centenario.

While playing against Bolivia, Christian became the youngest player to score for the United States. 

On September 2, 2016, the winger recorded two goals and an assist, becoming the youngest player to score in a World Cup qualifier representing the U.S. Also, he became the youngest athlete to score a brace in American history.

In the next match, Christian broke another youth record and became the youngest American player to start a World Cup qualifying match.

On top of that, Christian became the youngest player to captain the United States national team on November 20, 2018.

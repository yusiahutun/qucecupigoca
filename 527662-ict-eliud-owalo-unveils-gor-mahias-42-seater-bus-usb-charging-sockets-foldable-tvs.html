ICT CS Eliud Owalo Unveils Gor Mahia's New 42-Seater Bus with USB Charging Sockets, Foldable TVs

Didacus Malowa, a journalist at TUKO.co.ke, brings over three years of experience covering politics and current affairs in Kenya.

Nairobi - After years of dominating on the pitch, FKF-Premier League defending champions Gor Mahia FC launched a new team bus at Kasarani Stadium.

In a well-planned event, the modern 42-seater bus will leave the Maanzoni assembling plant for Kasarani in a convoy that would include the club's officials and supporters.

The event will be graced by my Information Communications and Digital Economy Cabinet Secretary, Eliud Owalo, who donated the bus following their exploits in the 2022/23 FKF-Premier League season.

Its launch will precede the much-hyped clash with rivals Murang’a SEAL, who have had a flying start to life in the top flight since their promotion from the National Super League.

Gor chairman Ambrose Rachier called on the club’s fans to turn up in large numbers and support the team as they face the Murang'a-based team.

Bragging rights will be up for grabs between the two betting giant Sportpesa-sponsored teams as SEALS sit behind Kogallo, as they are fondly referred to in the log on goal difference.

The builders explained that the Isuzu FVR 90 luxury bus with a 2x2 seat configuration has four VIP seats.

Each seat has reclining capability with USB charging socket ports, modern foldable TVs, and an immersive experience due to piped music and galaxy roof lighting.

Gor Mahia unveils trendy new kit

In November 2022, Gor Mahia announced the team would now wear intricately designed green and white shirts with flashes of green in customized print falling on the front bottom of the kit.

The shirts, designed by top sports equipment manufacturer Umbro, retail at KSh 2,500 per piece.

“We have never seen such a launch for any kit in Kenya and I am proud that our great club is the first to do so,” Gor Mahia chairman Ambrose Rachier told TUKO.co.ke.

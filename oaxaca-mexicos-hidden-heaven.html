Oaxaca: Mexico's Hidden Heaven of Breathtaking Landscapes

July 05, 2024
blog

Oaxaca is a place where nature shows off its amazing sights – a paradise of valleys, diverse plant and animal life, and grand mountains that capture your heart. This perfect spot combines the peace of green valleys with the magnificence of mountain views, creating a beautiful blend of nature. 

This beautiful place isn’t just lovely to look at; it’s a haven for nature lovers. Different environments, ranging from the seaside to the high mountains, are home to various plants and animals. Whether you’re in the valleys or the mountains, every part of this scenery displays a colorful variety of Mexico’s ecosystems.

In Oaxaca, nature is like a heaven and a playground. When you hike through the rough mountains, walk in the fertile valleys, and explore the different environments, each step reveals something beautiful. It’s a chance to see the wonders of a location where valleys, biodiversity, and mountains all come together in a perfect blend. This article is a quick journey to Oaxaca – a place with cool traditions, yummy foods, Spiritual festivals, weather, famous spots, and much more.

Cultural Heritage: A Tapestry Woven through Time

Oaxaca is a special place with lots of different cultures. People there speak different languages, and they keep old traditions alive. They have big celebrations, like the Day of the Dead, where they remember loved ones who passed away. It is famous for its talented artists who make colorful sculptures, clothes, and pottery. They also have ancient ruins at a place called Monte Albán, showing how clever and creative the people were a long time ago.

The food in Oaxaca is delicious, especially a sauce called mole. The people there mix their old beliefs with newer ones, creating a unique way of celebrating and worshiping. Every year, they have a big party called the Guelaguetza Festival, where they dance and wear bright costumes to share their diverse cultures with everyone. It is a special and lively place with a rich history and lots of things to celebrate.

Climate: Year-Round Pleasantness

It has a sub-tropical climate that is generally pleasant year-round, summers are rainy, with June as the wettest month. Spring is hot and dry, while winters are generally mild, experiencing little rain. Oaxaca’s weather offers diverse experiences, from lush summers to comfortable winters. The best times to visit this place are during the months of April to May and September to October. Both spring and fall are characterized by moderate temperatures and fewer tourists.

Geographical Range:

Mountainous Topography: Its Rocky Mountains are a beautiful and impressive sight for adventurous travelers. Whether you’re hiking in the Sierra Madre or exploring the peaks of the Mixteca region, the diverse mountains offer stunning views and a chance to connect with nature.

Lush Valleys: Oaxaca has valleys, such as Tlacolula and Ocotlán, where agriculture grows well. These lush landscapes, packed with greenery, highlight the local expertise in cultivating crops. During your visit, you’ll witness the cultivation of unique local products and engage yourself in the peaceful and traditional rural way of life.

Biodiversity:  Oaxaca has many different plants and animals, showing a lot of variety in its coastal and mountain areas. If you love nature, it is like a paradise, great for exploring Mexico’s different ecosystems.

A Culinary Journey:

Mole: Mole is a special Mexican sauce made from a variety of ingredients like chili peppers, fruits, nuts, seeds, and chocolate. There are many types of mole recipes found throughout Mexico, with Oaxaca having over 200 variations. Making authentic Oaxacan mole is a time-consuming process, often taking several days, but it’s considered a labor of love. The ingredients are toasted, ground, and simmered to create a thick and flavorful sauce. Mole is commonly served with rice and meat, such as chicken or pork, or used as a delicious sauce in dishes like enmoladas. It’s a unique and cherished part of Mexican culinary tradition.

Tlayudas: Tlayudas are like special Oaxacan pizzas that people love to eat, especially on the streets. Imagine a big, partly crispy tortilla with tasty beans, pork lard, and cheese on top – that’s a Tlayuda! It’s a bit like a pizza but with unique flavors. These yummy treats are a big deal in Oaxacan food, just like how everyone loves pizza. Originally from it, Tlayudas have become a popular snack in the city. Each bite is a mix of crunchy and delicious, telling a tasty story about the wonderful food in this place.

Memela: Memela is a yummy street food from this place, smaller than tlayudas. It’s like a small, crunchy disc made from masa, topped with tasty things like beans, salsa, chicken, guacamole, and fresh cheese. People in Oaxaca love it! You can also find memelas as popular snacks in other places like Guerrero and Puebla in Mexico. Depending on where you are, they might have different names like sope or huarache, but they’re all delicious masa treats with different toppings.

Enmoladas: Enmoladas are like a tasty version of enchiladas from here. They’re made by wrapping tortillas around yummy fillings and then covering them with a special dark sauce called mole negro. This sauce is what makes enmoladas different and delicious. People in Oaxaca often have them for breakfast, and you can find them in local markets. They’re like a flavorful Mexican morning treat with toppings like tasajo (cured beef) and queso fresco (fresh cheese) that add even more yumminess.

Day of the Dead: A Spiritual Celebration:

On the Day of the Dead, people believe that the spirits of their loved ones return to visit them. Children’s spirits come first, followed by adults, often guided by dogs. Instead of being a sad time, it’s a big celebration!

In the past, mourning death was seen as disrespectful in pre-Hispanic Mexico. People believed that death was just a physical separation, and mourning suggested that the departed were gone and no longer part of the community.

So, the Day of the Dead, or “Dia de los Muertos,” is a grand celebration of the lives of those who passed away. It’s a way to keep their memory alive, recognizing that death is a natural part of life’s cycle. It’s not sad or scary; instead, it’s a positive celebration with an incredible atmosphere!

Families visit the graves of their loved ones, making cemeteries glow with lights, flowers, and decorations. It’s truly a breathtaking sight! Families also create beautiful altars at home to honor the dead. These altars, adorned with candles, photographs, and the departed’s favorite food and drinks, serve to guide their spirits back home. These offerings provide sustenance as the spirits journey between worlds.

Top things to do in Oaxaca:

  • Huatulco National Park: Huatulco National Park, or Bahias de Huatulco National Park, is like a special nature area in Oaxaca, Mexico. The government declared it a National Park on July 24, 1998. It’s in Santa María Huatulco and is big, covering 11,890 hectares (29,400 acres).

This park is home to lots of different plants and animals. There are around 9,000 kinds of plants, 264 animals like armadillos and deer, and 701 types of birds including hummingbirds and pelicans. In the coastal areas, you’ll find mangroves and tall trees reaching 50 feet high.

People love visiting because they can see dolphins, whales, and turtles in the ocean. It’s like a special place where nature is protected, and everyone can enjoy its beauty.

  • Cascadas de Llano Grande: Cascadas de Llano Grande, or Llano Grande Waterfalls, is a beautiful spot in Oaxaca, Mexico. Here, stunning waterfalls create a peaceful and scenic environment, offering a refreshing escape. Surrounded by lush greenery, it’s a perfect place for nature lovers and those seeking a calming retreat. The sound of flowing water and the natural beauty make Cascadas de Llano Grande a memorable destination in Oaxaca.
  • La Crucecita: La Crucecita is a charming town in Oaxaca, Mexico, near Huatulco. It has a colorful square with the Church of La Virgen de Guadalupe, a lively market, and friendly streets filled with shops and cafes. The town is a great base for exploring nearby beaches and Huatulco National Park. With warm hospitality and a relaxed vibe, La Crucecita offers an authentic Mexican experience.
  • Maguey Bay: Maguey Bay, also known as Bahía Maguey, is a beautiful and peaceful coastal spot in this place, Mexico. With golden sands and clear waters surrounded by green hills, it’s a perfect place to relax. Visitors can enjoy activities like snorkeling and boat tours in this quiet and unspoiled bay, offering a serene escape along the coastline.
  • Monte Albán: Monte Albán in Oaxaca, Mexico, is an ancient city on a mountain plateau. Built by the Zapotec civilization, it reached its peak between 300 and 900 CE. The site features pyramids, temples, and ball courts, showcasing advanced architecture. With ceremonial plazas and stone reliefs like the Danzantes, Monte Albán offers insights into Zapotec culture. A UNESCO World Heritage site, it stands as a testament to Oaxaca’s rich history and the skills of its ancient inhabitants.

Safety Tips for Travelers: Embracing Cultural Sensitivity

Visitors to Oaxaca should be kind to local customs and traditions. It’s good to respect how people dress and behave for a happy trip. Also, knowing about health and safety tips keeps everyone well during the visit.

Conclusion: Timeless Glamor

As we finish our look at Oaxaca, it’s like a beautiful artwork full of rich culture, stunning nature, and deep spirituality. From the amazing mountains to the lively markets, every experience in this place takes you on a journey where old traditions mix with modern life. The Day of the Dead, with its special meaning, makes this place even more enchanting.

In every corner of Oaxaca, from lively festivals to mouth-watering culinary delights, a story unfolds. The region’s commitment to sustainable tourism ensures that its treasures endure, promising an immersive experience for generations to come. It is not just a destination; it’s an invitation to delve into the soul of Mexico, where every street corner tells a story, every festival echoes ancient traditions, and every dish served is a culinary masterpiece. As you explore the wonders of this place, you embark on an immersive journey into the heart and spirit of this extraordinary place.

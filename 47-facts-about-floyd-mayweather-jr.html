Mayweather’s full name is Floyd Joy Mayweather Jr.

Floyd Mayweather Jr. shares the same name as his father, who was a professional boxer and competed in the welterweight division.

He was born in Grand Rapids, Michigan.

Mayweather hails from Grand Rapids, a city located in the western part of the state of Michigan.

Mayweather comes from a family of boxers.

His father, Floyd Mayweather Sr., was a boxer, as well as his uncles Jeff Mayweather and Roger Mayweather, who also became boxing trainers.

He started boxing at a young age.

Mayweather began his boxing career at the age of 7 and quickly displayed his natural talent and passion for the sport.

Mayweather won a bronze medal at the 1996 Olympic Games.

Representing the United States, Mayweather competed in the featherweight division at the 1996 Atlanta Olympics and narrowly missed out on a gold or silver medal, earning a bronze.

He turned professional in 1996.

Shortly after the Olympics, Mayweather decided to turn professional and made his debut as a pro boxer on October 11, 1996, against Roberto Apodaca.

Mayweather has won world titles in five different weight classes.

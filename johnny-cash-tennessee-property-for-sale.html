Johnny Cash Home for Sale

August 08, 2024
blog

Want a home like Johnny and June? Well, now you can own their actual home. The lakefront property in Hendersonville, Tennessee, outside Nashville, where Johnny Cash and June Carter Cash formerly lived, is officially on the market.

The listing price for the 4 1/2-acre estate at 200 Caudill Drive is $1. Not so fast: That just means current owner, businessman James Gresham, hasn't pinpointed a specific price he wants to sell it for. But Vice President of realty company Crye-Leike Realtors Stan Peacock tells CountryLiving.com that only offers over $3 million will be considered.

Stan Peacock

After all, it's pretty much priceless. It's the same property where Johnny and June spent their entire married life—from the time they tied the knot in the late '60s to their deaths in 2003—and where the legendary country star hosted musicians (Bob Dylan, Brooks & Dunn), politicians (President Ronald Reagan, Al Gore), and more (evangelist Billy Graham). It's also the very place that inspired Johnny's daughter, Rosanne Cash, to write "House on the Lake" after her father died. "In my mind [the next owner] would be some person [who] is a huge Johnny Cash fan," Gresham told The Wall Street Journal.

Stan Peacock

"Well, I miss the sounds of Tennessee And the smell of heavy rain The roses in the garden Laugh before the pain But I hear his voice close in my ear I see her smile and wave I blink and while my eyes are closed They both have gone away." —Rosanne Cash, "House on the Lake"

Stan Peacock

While the 13,880-square-foot house itself was destroyed by a fire during a remodel in 2007, stone walls, a fireplace, and a staircase remain intact, as well as a garage ...

Stan Peacock

...a swimming pool ...

Stan Peacock

... a tennis court, and June's bell garden.

Stan Peacock

June's dressing room was converted into a modern apartment, where, as the listing suggests, buyers can live "while your dream is being built."

Stan Peacock

Stan Peacock

"Details are everywhere to be seen with memories galore," says the listing—including a tree carved with Johnny Cash's face. There are also wooden beams that survived the fire and could be used in building a new home, says Peacock.

Stan Peacock

Prior to Gresham, the estate was purchased for $2.3 million by Barry Gibb of the Bee Gees, who had hoped to restore the home and live and write songs in it with his wife, Linda, but it caught fire before they ever could. As Peacock pointed out to us, "the opportunity to actually own the Johnny Cash property might only be available once in our lifetimes."

Interested in putting in an offer? Contact Stan Peacock, Vice President of Crye-Leike Realtors, at peacocks@crye-leike.com, or call 615-512-3848.

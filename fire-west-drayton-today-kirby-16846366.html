Massive fire rips through London building 70 firefighters tackling blaze

May 19, 2024
blog

Around 70 firefighters and 15 fire engines are tackling the blaze on the Kirby Estate in West Drayton.

Plumes of smoke and flames have been seen for miles around after the fire broke out at around 11.36am today (Saturday, 3 February).

A spokesman for London Fire Brigade told Get West London that 75% of the industrial estate was ablaze earlier and that fire crews are still on the scene battling the inferno.

Witness Ethan Pearce, 15, said: "I was walking into Iceland in Yiewsley and saw a huge black cloud rising from what looks to be like an industrial estate, there were at least four fire engines along Trout Road which is blocked off by police."

Another local resident Lukasz Chmura added: "I saw lots of thick, black smoke from our house – it smelt horrible."

The industrial estate – close to the High Street – is under three miles from Heathrow Airport and just a mile from the M4 and M25 motorways.

Trout Road is still closed both ways at Wraysbury Drive, and the AA reports that traffic is very heavy in the area due to smoke.

Public transport in the area is being diverted and the High Street and Aldi store have been closed.

A spokesman for Scotland Yard said: "There are no reports of injuries."

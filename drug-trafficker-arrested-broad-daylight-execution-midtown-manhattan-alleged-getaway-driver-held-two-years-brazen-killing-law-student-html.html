Lloyd McKenzie arrested over Brandon Woodard's murder in Midtown Manhattan

April 15, 2024
blog

The alleged getaway driver involved in the brazen 2012 execution-style shooting of a Los Angeles man on a busy Manhattan street has been arrested.

Lloyd McKenzie, 37, was arraigned Wednesday morning in state Supreme Court on murder and other charges related to Brandon Woodard's death.

'Three years ago, Brandon Woodard was murdered on a Midtown sidewalk in broad daylight,' said Manhattan District Attorney Cyrus Vance, Jr. 

'This brazen shooting, stemming from a major interstate drug conspiracy, shocked and frightened many at the time.

'I would like to thank the prosecutors and investigators in my office and the NYPD for their dedication to this case, and for their continuing pursuit of those responsible.'

McKenzie allegedly owed over $160,000 for cocaine that was supplied to him by 31-year-old Woodard, according to the indictment, the CBS New York reported.

After Woodard, who was a father-of-one and law student, arrived to New York City to collect money owed on December 10, 2012, McKenzie and the unknown gunman allegedly lured Woodard to West 58th Street. 

Police said that surveillance footage from the incident shows the suspect, who appears to have a beard and be bald, getting out of a car about 10 minutes before the shooting and pulling a hood over his head on his jacket.  

Manhattan District Attorney Cyrus Vance, Jr said: 'Three years ago, Brandon Woodard (above) was murdered on a Midtown sidewalk in broad daylight. 'This brazen shooting, stemming from a major interstate drug conspiracy, shocked and frightened many at the time'

The gunman waited for Woodard, who was looking down at his cell phone, to walk by. Moments later, the suspect walked up behind Woodard and pulled out a semi-automatic pistol, firing one single shot at his head, police said. 

He walked to the corner of Seventh Avenue and 58th Street, where he got into the passenger seat of a waiting Lincoln MKZ, that police say McKenzie was driving.

McKenzie has been charged with second-degree murder, operating as a major trafficker, conspiracy, criminal possession of a weapon and criminal possession of a controlled substance, CBS New York reported. 

He is due back in court on March 15. 

District Attorney Cyrus R. Vance Jr says investigators will continue their search for suspects.

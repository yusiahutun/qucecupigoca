Meet 8-year-old Graham-Henries, the youngest black barber in the world

February 07, 2024
blog

- Neijae Graham-Henries is reportedly the world's youngest barber

- The 8-year-old jostles schools and giving free haircuts to the needy and at the same time mastering her skills from Philadelphia barbers

- Graham-Henries won World Record Academy title of 'World’s Youngest Barber for her passion and skill

At age seven, Neijae Graham-Henries found her passion when her brother chose to discontinue his studies at the Junior Barber Academy in North Philadelphia in 2018.

Even as a toddler, Neijae believed people can show others something that they are capable of doing extraordinarily.

Face2Face Africa reported that her mother, Jamie Graham, revealed the young girl was not intimidated despite being the youngest female student.

''She wasn’t intimidated because she was the youngest student, and she was not intimidated because she was the only female'' Jamie said.

Before starting her second grade, Graham-Henries had become a certified barber.

Soon after her training, the young girl started volunteering to cut students’ hair for free at a back-to-school event.

The 8-year-old spends her time going to school and giving free haircuts to those in need and at the same time mastering her skills from Philadelphia barbers.

Describing her own technique, Graham-Henries said: ''The whole process is to finish it and do it really good and to, like, style it good.''

Graham-Henries received international attention from outlets like BuzzFeed and Inside Edition when footage of her working went viral on social media.

She also got invited to speak at the Essence Beauty Carnival in New York City.

At this young age, Graham-Henries is confident about her career in a male-dominated profession and her resolve to pursue her passion has won her the World Record Academy title of 'World’s Youngest Barber.

Even though Graham-Henries has styled a few women, she enjoys cutting men’s hair.

''They have a better texture,'' she said, adding, ''no offense,'' she added.

She can secure a barber’s license in Pennsylvania when she gets to 16.

In the meantime, Graham-Henries must provide free haircuts under the watchful eye of a licensed barber.

The youngster does not just enjoy cutting hair, she said she enjoys sleeping, eating, dancing, and playing video games like Minecraft.

She is also envisaging a career in sewing as she pursues her barber’s license.

Her dream is to have Kevin Hart or LeBron James as clients in the future.

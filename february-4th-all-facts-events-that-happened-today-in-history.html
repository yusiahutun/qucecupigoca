HISTORICAL EVENTS

  • 1789: George Washington is unanimously elected as the first President of the United States.
  • 1913: The Sixteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution, allowing the federal government to impose and collect income taxes, is ratified.
  • 1945: The Yalta Conference begins, with leaders of the Allied powers meeting to discuss the post-World War II reorganization of Germany and other European countries.
  • 1974: Patricia Hearst, granddaughter of newspaper magnate William Randolph Hearst, is kidnapped by the radical left-wing group, the Symbionese Liberation Army.
  • 2004: Facebook, the social networking website founded by Mark Zuckerberg, is launched from his Harvard University dormitory room.

SCIENTIFIC BREAKTHROUGHS

  • 1677: The first medical publication, the Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society, is published in London.
  • 1808: Lithium, the lightest metal and an important element in batteries, is discovered by Sir Humphry Davy.
  • 1932: The neutron, a subatomic particle with no net electric charge, is discovered by James Chadwick.
  • 1969: The first test flight of the Boeing 747, the iconic jumbo jet, takes place.
  • 2004: The Mars rover Opportunity lands on the surface of Mars, beginning its mission to explore the red planet.

POLITICAL MILESTONES

  • 1787: The Tordesillas Treaty is signed, dividing the newly discovered lands outside Europe between the Spanish and Portuguese empires.
  • 1938: Adolf Hitler becomes the commander-in-chief of the German armed forces, consolidating his power in Nazi Germany.
  • 1997: A peace treaty is signed in Guatemala, putting an end to the 36-year-long Guatemalan Civil War.
  • 2004: The Republic of Georgia officially joins NATO’s Partnership for Peace program.
  • 2011: The uprising in Egypt, known as the Arab Spring, reaches its peak as massive protests demand the resignation of President Hosni Mubarak.

CULTURAL EVENTS

  • 1789: Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart’s last symphony, Symphony No. 41, also known as the “Jupiter Symphony,” is premiered.
  • 1938: Disney’s animated film “Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs” is released, marking the studio’s first full-length animated feature.
  • 1964: The Beatles arrive in the United States for their first American visit, kicking off the “British Invasion” of popular music.
  • 2007: The Broadway musical “Wicked” becomes the third-longest-running show in Broadway history.
  • 2018: The Philadelphia Eagles win their first-ever Super Bowl, defeating the New England Patriots in Super Bowl LII.

BIRTHS

  • 1913: Rosa Parks, American civil rights activist who sparked the Montgomery Bus Boycott.
  • 1948: Alice Cooper, American rock singer known for his theatrical stage shows.
  • 1962: Clint Black, American country music singer-songwriter.
  • 1975: Natalie Imbruglia, Australian singer-songwriter and actress.
  • 1986: Hannibal Buress, American comedian and actor.

NOTABLE DEATHS

  • 1199: King Richard I of England, commonly known as Richard the Lionheart.
  • 1902: Mary Todd Lincoln, the wife of U.S. President Abraham Lincoln.
  • 2006: Betty Friedan, American feminist and author of “The Feminine Mystique.”
  • 2018: John Mahoney, British-American actor known for his role as Martin Crane in the TV show “Frasier.”
  • 2020: Kirk Douglas, American actor and producer, known for films such as “Spartacus” and “Paths of Glory.”

Conclusion

February 4th has been witness to numerous significant events throughout history. From political milestones such as the election of George Washington as the first President of the United States, to scientific breakthroughs like the discovery of lithium and the neutron, this date holds great historical importance.

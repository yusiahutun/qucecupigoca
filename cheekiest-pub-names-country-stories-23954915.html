Cheekiest pub names in the country and stories behind hilarious and dirty monikers

June 26, 2024
blog

If you're an avid pub punter in England, chances are you drink in a Royal Oak, Crown, White Hart or King's Head.

These are among the most popular names for watering holes across the country and it's easy to see why with their traditional, pastoral and generally reassuring qualities.

But what if your local sounded more like a sex act than a bar? Or despite being actually innocent evoked a work long shut out of polite society?

As part of our Great British Booze Off competition, we're pointing you in the direction of some of the rudest, daftest and downright dirty names of UK pubs.

And as with other GBBO articles, we're inviting you to crown the best establishments in the country by voting in our poll below before May 31st.

With cash prizes available to the winners, now's your chance to celebrate your favourite free house after a year of industry-shattering coronavirus restrictions!

So while you're chuckling at the ridiculous names below, take a moment to vote in our competition and feel free to check out our other Great British Booze Off articles to find the hidden gems you may well have missed.

The Polished Knob, Todmorden, West Yorkshire

When we first spotted this delightful pub in a picturesque countryside setting, we assumed that it was Christened as such in a far-gone time when 'knob' could be considered slang for male genitalia.

But the strange thing about this name is that isn't even the original one. It used to be called the Black Swan before being reborn to bear this hilarious title in 2011.

Real Ales are on offer for as little as £2 and it also doubles-up as hotel for anyone who wants to stay over in the stunning town of Todmorden.

The Legend Of Oily Johnnies, Winscales, Cumbria

Never has the name of a pub offered such a false picture of the services it offers. Rather than offering up stories about greasy prophylactics, staff serve up delicious dishes that wouldn't look out of place in a top restaurant.

As with the Polished Knob, this boozer had a considerably less colourful name when it was previously known as the Royal Oak.

It's now named after James Kirkpatrick, a previous landlord who used to sell paraffin oil from a shed next to the pub and became known locally as Oily Johnnie.

Moody Cow, Upton Bishop, Ross-on-Wye

Animal names are a common theme across British pubs. But they usually don't resemble an insult thrown during a domestic row.

Nonetheless, the Moody Cow has enjoyed enormous success being listed in the Michelin Red Guide for the last 7 years.

If you're a fan of Sunday roasts (who isn't?) then it's worth knowing this place has been voted in the top 10 for roast dinners in Herefordshire. And the Guardian put it in the UK's top 50 for everyone's favourite traditional dish.

The Foul Mouthed Count, Dorset

Passers-by took one look at this pub's sign and knew what need to be done.

Not only does it now bear an incredibly rude name, but the fuming bloke pictured isn't exactly reassuring.

So proud are the owners of its new moniker, that they proudly call themselves The Foul Mouthed C*** on social media when linking to their official website.

The Rank Turd, Kidderminster, West Midlands

This beauty of a name-change was the work of a brazen builder who was refurbishing the Grand Turk.

Paul Manning, 42, couldn't resist rejigging the name while he was working on the 165-year-old boozer, the Birmingham Mail reported at the time.

The owners were swift to revert the pub to its proper name, but it still makes our list for the sheer cheek of the labourer.

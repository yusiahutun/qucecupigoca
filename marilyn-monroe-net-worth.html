Monroe’s Career and Fame

Marilyn Monroe had a remarkable career, leaving an indelible mark on the world of entertainment. With 33 acting credits to her name, Monroe became one of the most prominent figures in Hollywood during her short but impactful career.

In her films, Monroe showcased her exceptional talent and captivating charm, captivating audiences with her unforgettable performances. She embodied the quintessential “blonde bombshell” and became an iconic symbol of beauty and allure.

Monroe starred in several noteworthy films that propelled her to fame. Some of her most notable roles include:

  • “Gentlemen Prefer Blondes” – In this 1953 musical comedy, Monroe played the role of Lorelei Lee, a showgirl determined to marry a wealthy man.
  • “Some Like It Hot” – Monroe’s portrayal of Sugar Kane Kowalczyk in this 1959 comedy won critical acclaim and solidified her status as a talented actress.
  • “The Seven Year Itch” – Monroe’s iconic scene standing over a subway grate and her unforgettable rendition of “Happy Birthday, Mr. President” to John F. Kennedy showcased her star power.

    • Monroe’s films captivated audiences and propelled her to the ranks of the highest-paid celebrities in Hollywood. Her talent, beauty, and undeniable charisma endeared her to fans worldwide.

    Monroe’s Estate and Inheritance

    In her will, Marilyn Monroe carefully planned the distribution of her estate, ensuring that her loved ones and important causes would be provided for. She left specific instructions for the allocation of her assets and intellectual property.

    To her longtime assistant and half-sister, Monroe bequeathed $10,000 each, showing her appreciation for their support and companionship. Additionally, she established a $5,000 trust fund for the education of her assistant’s child, highlighting her commitment to nurturing future generations.

    A substantial amount of Monroe’s estate was dedicated to her mother’s well-being. She set up a $100,000 trust fund to ensure her mother’s care, reflecting her love and responsibility towards her family.

    The majority of Monroe’s intellectual property and estate—75 percent—was left to her acting coach, Lee Strasberg. Recognizing Strasberg’s influence and guidance throughout her career, Monroe entrusted him with her legacy and intellectual pursuits.

    The remaining 25 percent of Monroe’s estate was assigned to her therapist, Dr. Marianne Kris, who played a vital role in supporting her emotional well-being. Following Dr. Kris’s passing, a portion of her share was transferred to the Anna Freud Centre in London, furthering the cause of mental health.

    Leveraging her intellectual property and image rights, Lee Strasberg’s second wife, Anna Mizrahi, inherited Monroe’s estate. Mizrahi assumed the responsibility of managing Monroe’s likeness and image rights, using her business acumen to create lucrative licensing deals and endorsement opportunities. Through her efforts, Monroe’s brand became one of the most profitable among deceased celebrities.

    Monroe’s Home and Valuable Assets

    Marilyn Monroe’s most valuable asset was her home in the Brentwood neighborhood of Los Angeles. She purchased the property in January 1962 for $77,500, using a mortgage that required monthly payments of $320. This was the only home Monroe ever owned. The home was sold for $7.25 million in 2017.

    Monroe also possessed valuable assets such as jewelry, clothing, and personal effects. However, upon her death, these assets were sold off in auctions, including the famous peach-and-rhinestone dress she wore to sing “Happy Birthday, Mr. President” to John F. Kennedy.

    Monroe’s Death and Funeral

    Marilyn Monroe’s untimely death on August 5, 1962, shocked the world and left an indelible mark on Hollywood history. The cause of her death was determined to be acute barbiturate poisoning, and it was ruled as a probable suicide. Monroe was found lifeless in her Brentwood home at the age of 36, plunging fans into mourning and raising many questions about the circumstances surrounding the beloved actress’s demise.

While it was not a grand spectacle, reflecting the financial strain on her estate, it marked the final farewell to a Hollywood legend. The world bid farewell to Monroe, an iconic figure who captivated hearts with her enchanting presence and undeniable talent.

    Ownership and Exploitation of Monroe’s Image and Likeness

    After Lee Strasberg’s wife, Anna Mizrahi, inherited 75 percent of Marilyn Monroe’s estate, she gained control over Monroe’s image and likeness. Mizrahi capitalized on this opportunity by entering into numerous licensing deals, allowing for the use of Monroe’s signature and image on various products and endorsements. These licensing deals included collaborations with well-known brands such as Mercedes-Benz, Revlon, and Coca-Cola.

    Mizrahi partnered with CMG Worldwide, a renowned company specializing in managing the estates of deceased celebrities, to handle the licensing of Monroe’s brand. Through these strategic partnerships, Mizrahi transformed Monroe into one of the highest-earning deceased celebrities, generating millions of dollars in licensing revenue.

    The exploitation of Monroe’s image rights and likeness not only contributed to the monetization of her legacy but also ensured the preservation and continued relevance of her iconic status in popular culture.

    Monroe’s Personal Struggles and the Search for Identity

    Marilyn Monroe’s life was not all glitz and glamour. Behind the iconic smile and the voluptuous figure, she faced personal struggles that often went unnoticed by the public. Monroe battled with mental health issues, including depression and anxiety, which greatly impacted her well-being and career. The pressure to maintain her image as the quintessential sex symbol of the 1950s added to her challenges, leaving her longing for a sense of personal identity beyond the public persona.

    A documentary titled “The Mystery of Marilyn Monroe: The Unheard Tapes” sheds light on Monroe’s inner thoughts and her relentless quest to find herself amidst the objectification of her image and identity by the world. In these unheard tapes, Monroe speaks candidly about her desires for authenticity and self-expression, offering a rare glimpse into the depth of her personal struggles.
